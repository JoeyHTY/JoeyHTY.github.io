Research Interests

I am deeply engaged in high-energy astrophysics, particularly focusing on Active Galactic Nuclei (AGNs) and the mechanisms driving their energetic phenomena. My primary research involves MHD (Magnetohydrodynamic) simulations to understand the dynamics of magnetically driven winds from accretion disks surrounding black holes. This research aims to investigate the role of poloidal magnetic fields (Bp) in transporting mass and energy away from the disk, thus contributing to the feedback processes influencing galaxy evolution.

Additionally, I am working on projects related to dark matter detection, specifically simulating and predicting the sensitivity of dark matter detectors. These simulations help in understanding the potential interactions of dark matter particles with standard model particles, thus contributing to the broader search for dark matter in the universe.

Current Projects

1. Magnetically Driven Winds from Accretion Disks:
Investigating the efficiency of Bp in driving winds from black hole accretion disks. Studying how the magnetic field configuration influences the amount of material transported by these winds.

2. Dark Matter Detector Simulations:
Simulating the performance and sensitivity of dark matter detectors. Analyzing the potential detection rates and interaction cross-sections of dark matter particles.
